Our society is becoming increasingly dependent on advanced technological systems. For example, space vehicles and high-altitude aircraft rely on sophisticated, yet delicate, computer systems both for safety and for navigation. Global communications depend on high-frequency (or HF), very high frequency (or VHF), and ultra high frequency (or UHF) radio signals. Power distribution relies on an interconnected network of lines, transformers, computers, and other devices. As a result, we need to be aware of the potential effects of geomagnetic storms on their ability to function. Geomagnetic storms occur when a mass of plasma is ejected from the Sun and strikes the Earth and its surroundings. This plasma "bubble" travels away from the Sun at about two million miles per hour, and follows the three-dimensional spiral pattern of the Sun's magnetic field. It can reach Earth in about 40 hours.
